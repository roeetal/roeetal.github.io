About me

I created this website not only because I wanted to learn web development but also because I have a deep interest in technology and wanted to share my passion and experiences with other people.

A part of me often plays with the thought of how we, as humans, would be able to expand our abilities through innovative technologies. I was recently hiking (which I must add is essential for anyone visiting Vancouver) and noticed my feet strategically sliding off roots and small rocks. And then the idea of a robot, a hiking rescue robot which could perform similar strategic calculations, came to mind. With such dynamic controls, imagine how much closer we would be to emulating the complexities of the human brain, and to applying these complexities to the development of more advanced robots. My passion is to combine my expanding knowledge in mathematics and physics with my creativity and unique perspectives, with the hope of developing groundbreaking innovations. I am currently focusing on learning about solid-sttae physics, so that someday I can contribute to the development of the first scalable quantum computer.

There is also this other part of me: and this part of me like to think of us, humans. How we think; why we do the things that we do; how we behave as individuals and as communities. In my ideal world, I will spend the next couple of years working on the aforementioned, and maybe even try merge the worlds of mathematics and physics with that of philosophy. Hopefully the content on this website will aptly communicate how my journey is going...
